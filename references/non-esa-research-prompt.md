# Handoff: Non-ESA State Funding Programs — 33 Jurisdictions

**Objective:** Research education funding programs available to homeschool families in the 33 states/jurisdictions that do NOT have an active Education Savings Account (ESA) program. The user wants to find "anything useful as far as money or reimbursements — your state doesn't have an ESA but they do have a discount program or you can apply for this thing."

**Scope:** 50 states + DC. Exclude the 19 states already covered by the active ESA directory. This covers the remaining 31 + DC = 32 jurisdictions, plus a few edge states where programs are vouchers/tax-credits rather than ESAs.

## Core Categories of Non-ESA Programs

When visiting each state, look for any of these program types:

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Voucher** | Public funds follow student to private school | OH EdChoice, WI MPCP |
| **Tax-credit scholarship** | Businesses/individuals donate to SGOs, get tax credit, fund scholarships | PA EITC, VA EISC |
| **Refundable tax credit** | Parent claims credit on tax return for approved expenses | OK Parental Choice ($1K homeschool) |
| **Tax deduction** | Deduct education expenses from taxable income | MN K-12 Subtraction |
| **Income-based tax credit** | Refundable/nonrefundable credit; income-limited | MN K-12 Education Credit |
| **Correspondence allotment** | Public correspondence school + per-student funding | AK CSAP ($4,500) |
| **Town tuitioning** | District pays tuition to approved school if no local school | ME, VT |
| **Federal EFTC** | Federal tax credit scholarship (starts Jan 1, 2027) | All opted-in states |
| **Special-needs only** | Narrowly targeted, but real money if eligible | OH Jon Peterson, IN ESA |
| **None / de minimis** | No homeschool-accessible funding | Several states |

## States to Research

### Tier 1 — Active Non-ESA Programs (definite money if eligible)

These states have operational programs that homeschool families can access. Visit the program page and extract details.

#### 1. AK — Alaska
- **Program:** Correspondence School Allotment Program (CSAP)
- **URL:** https://education.alaska.gov/compass/ParentPortal/ExploreOptions
- **Amount:** Up to $4,500 per student
- **Type:** Public correspondence school allotment (not an ESA but functionally similar)
- **What to extract:**
  - Is the allotment available to any homeschool family, or only those enrolled in specific correspondence programs?
  - Are there approved expense lists?
  - How is reimbursement handled?
  - Any legislative changes pending? (2026 Senate committee action on CSAP reform)

#### 2. DC — District of Columbia
- **Program:** D.C. Opportunity Scholarship Program
- **URL:** https://servingourchildrendc.org/
- **Amount:** Up to $10,000 K-8, up to $15,000 9-12
- **Type:** Federal voucher
- **What to extract:**
  - Can homeschool families access this, or is it limited to private school enrollment?
  - Income caps? Application windows?
  - 2025 reauthorization status?

#### 3. ME — Maine
- **Program:** Town Tuitioning Program
- **URL:** https://www.maine.gov/doe/school-choice/town-tuitioning
- **Amount:** Tuition to approved public/private school
- **Type:** Town tuitioning (district-funded)
- **What to extract:**
  - Can homeschool families access any school choice funding?
  - Is there a homeschool-specific funding pathway?
  - JHU Homeschool Hub flagged "could potentially expand, opening up funding for homeschool families" — any update?

#### 4. MN — Minnesota
- **Program A:** K-12 Education Subtraction
- **URL:** https://www.revenue.state.mn.us/k-12-education-subtraction-and-credit
- **Amount:** Up to $1,500 per child (deduction from taxable income), family cap if 2+ kids
- **Type:** Tax deduction — directly available to homeschool families

- **Program B:** K-12 Education Credit
- **URL:** https://www.revenue.state.mn.us/k-12-education-subtraction-and-credit
- **Amount:** Up to $1,500 per child refundable tax credit (income-limited)
- **Type:** Refundable tax credit

- **What to extract:**
  - Exact eligible expenses list for homeschool families
  - Income phase-out thresholds
  - Do families need receipts? What documentation?
  - Is the credit refundable (i.e., can you get it even if you owe $0 tax)?
  - What do these two programs combined actually mean for a family spending $3,000 on curriculum?

#### 5. NE — Nebraska
- **Program A:** Opportunity Scholarships of Nebraska (existing tax-credit scholarship)
- **URL:** https://www.nebraskaopportunity.org/
- **Type:** Tax-credit scholarship via SGOs
- **Program B:** EFTC opt-in (Jan 2027)
- **Amount:** Up to $1,700 federal credit per donor per student
- **What to extract:**
  - Can homeschool families apply for existing Opportunity Scholarships?
  - What's the SGO application process look like?
  - EFTC effective date and any state-level implementation details

#### 6. OH — Ohio
- **Program A:** EdChoice Expansion Scholarship
- **URL:** https://education.ohio.gov/Topics/Other-Resources/Scholarships/EdChoice-Expansion
- **Amount:** $6,166 K-8, $8,408 9-12
- **Type:** Voucher (requires private school enrollment)

- **Program B:** Jon Peterson Special Needs Scholarship
- **URL:** https://education.ohio.gov/Topics/Other-Resources/Scholarships/Jon-Peterson-Special-Needs-Scholarship
- **Amount:** Up to $27,000+ depending disability
- **Type:** Voucher (IEP required, ages 3-21)

- **Program C:** Autism Scholarship
- **URL:** https://education.ohio.gov/Topics/Other-Resources/Scholarships/Autism-Scholarship
- **Amount:** Varies
- **Type:** Voucher (autism diagnosis required)

- **What to extract:**
  - Do any of these allow parent-directed spending (homeschool-ish use)?
  - Jon Peterson — can funds go to parent-selected providers?
  - What documentation is needed?
  - Application windows for 2026-27

#### 7. OK — Oklahoma
- **Program:** Parental Choice Tax Credit
- **URL:** https://oklahoma.gov/tax/individuals/parental-choice-tax-credit.html
- **Amount:** Up to $1,000 per student for homeschool; $5,000-$7,500 for private school
- **Type:** Refundable tax credit
- **What to extract:**
  - Exactly what receipts must be kept? What qualifies as "homeschool educational expenses"?
  - How is the credit claimed (Form 591-D)?
  - Application vs. claiming on return — what's the process?
  - Deadline/program cap?

#### 8. PA — Pennsylvania
- **Program A:** Educational Improvement Tax Credit (EITC)
- **URL:** https://dced.pa.gov/programs/educational-improvement-tax-credit-program-eitc/
- **Amount:** Scholarships up to $40,000+ depending grade/school/family size
- **Type:** Tax-credit scholarship via SGOs

- **Program B:** Opportunity Scholarship Tax Credit (OSTC)
- **URL:** https://dced.pa.gov/programs/opportunity-scholarship-tax-credit-program-ostc/
- **Amount:** Similar structure to EITC
- **Type:** Tax-credit scholarship (attendance-zone specific)

- **What to extract:**
  - Can homeschool families apply for EITC scholarships? (Most EITC is for private school, but PA has unusual homeschool law — registered homeschool vs. private school tutors)
  - Application windows for 2026-27
  - Income limits? Waitlist status?
  - Any homeschool-specific SGOs?

#### 9. VA — Virginia
- **Program:** Education Improvement Scholarships Tax Credits
- **URL:** https://www.doe.virginia.gov/data-policy-funding/school-finance/education-improvement-scholarships-tax-credits-program
- **Amount:** Varies by SGO
- **Type:** Tax-credit scholarship via SGOs

- **What to extract:**
  - Can homeschool families participate in EISTC?
  - Are there SGOs that serve homeschool families?
  - Income caps?
  - Also: VA opted into EFTC for Jan 2027

#### 10. WI — Wisconsin
- **Program A:** Milwaukee Parental Choice Program (MPCP) / Racine / Statewide WPCP
- **URL:** https://dpi.wi.gov/parental-education-options/choice-programs/student-applications
- **Amount:** $10,877 K-8, $13,371 9-12 (2025-26)
- **Type:** Voucher (requires private school enrollment)

- **Program B:** Special Needs Scholarship Program (SNSP)
- **URL:** https://dpi.wi.gov/parental-education-options/special-needs-scholarship
- **Amount:** ~$16,049 estimated (2025-26)
- **Type:** Voucher/scholarship (special needs)

- **What to extract:**
  - Can homeschool families access either program?
  - SNSP — does it allow parent-directed spending? What qualifies?
  - Would a homeschool family need to enroll in a private school to use these?
  - EFTC was vetoed by Gov. Evers — confirm status

### Tier 2 — EFTC Opt-In States (future money, Jan 2027+)

These states have opted into the federal Education Freedom Tax Credit but have NO other state-funded homeschool program. Starting Jan 1, 2027, families in these states can receive scholarships funded by federal tax credits.

For each, note: has the governor submitted the SGO list to Treasury? What's the current implementation stage?

- **CO** — Colorado (opted in; also has some existing choice programs)
- **ID** — Idaho (no other programs)
- **KS** — Kansas (opted in; verify if any existing state tax-credit scholarship still active)
- **KY** — Kentucky (previous EOA struck down; EFTC opted in for 2027)
- **MT** — Montana (ESA blocked in court; EFTC opted in)
- **ND** — North Dakota (ESA bill vetoed; EFTC opted in)
- **NV** — Nevada (ESA law exists but unfunded; EFTC opted in)
- **SD** — South Dakota (no other programs)
- **VT** — Vermont (town tuitioning exists but not for homeschool; EFTC opted in)

## Cross-Cutting: The EFTC Program (Starting Jan 1, 2027)

This is the biggest emerging opportunity. Research the **federal Education Freedom Tax Credit (IRC §25F)** at:

- https://eccacredit.com/states
- https://federalscholarships.com/
- https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R48724

**What to understand:**
- Individual donors can claim up to $1,700/year federal tax credit for donations to SGOs
- SGOs then award scholarships to K-12 students
- **30 states opted in** (30 governors submitted SGO lists to Treasury)
- How do families *find and apply* for these scholarships? Through which SGOs?
- Is it purely private school, or can homeschool families be served?
- What's the practical application workflow for a family?
- What's the competitive landscape — which SGOs serve homeschoolers specifically?

## Research method

1. Visit the official state program URL for each Tier 1 state first
2. Visit SGO/administrator portal pages where available
3. Check EdChoice program page for each state (https://www.edchoice.org/school-choice/programs/)
4. Check JHU Homeschool Hub for each state (https://education.jhu.edu/edpolicy/policy-research-initiatives/homeschool-hub/states/)
5. For EFTC states: visit https://eccacredit.com/states for updated implementation status
6. For "nothing here" states: confirm with at least one search, then tag as confirmed none
7. HSLDA member portal may have detailed per-state program info (member-only access may limit this)

## Risk notes

- **OH, WI vouchers** — These require enrollment in a participating private school. A homeschool family would need to decide if partial enrollment is practical.
- **PA EITC** — The PA homeschool law is unique (registered homeschoolers vs. private school tutors). Verify whether EITC serves homeschool families through specific SGOs.
- **MN Subtraction + Credit** — These stack! A family can claim both. This is one of the most homeschool-friendly tax setups in the country.
- **OK tax credit** — Refundable means cash in hand even with zero tax liability. $1,000/student is real money for curriculum.
- **AK CSAP** — Not exactly a homeschool program (correspondence school enrollment required), but functionally similar to an ESA for many families.
- **EFTC launch (Jan 2027)** — Completely changes the landscape for 30 states. Any product positioning should explicitly address what changes in 2027.
- **NE Opportunity Scholarship** — Different from EFTC; verify whether Nebraska families can currently access tax-credit scholarships, or if the state is waiting for the federal program to start.

## Conflict handling (from B4 research methodology)

1. Official current state portal wins for parent action URLs
2. State statute/regulation wins for durable legal requirements
3. EdChoice/NCSL for program existence and cross-state comparison
4. EFTC tracker (eccacredit.com) for federal program participation
5. JHU Homeschool Hub for homeschool-specific program notes
6. Parent/forum claims as low-confidence unless backed by official source behavior
